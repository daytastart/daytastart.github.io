// Featured project · Work simulation · Jun 2026

A strategy to double Shopee's pickup adoption — built by testing the obvious answer first

A Singapore beachhead strategy for Shopee's offline pickup service: diagnose why adoption sits at ~10%, allocate US$10M over 5 years, and build a playbook that scales to Southeast Asia.

The plot twist

"Just build more lockers" — disproved.

Singapore's Pick Network already blankets the island: 1,000+ lockers, 24/7, within 250m of every HDB block — and still runs at ~13% utilisation. Supply was never the constraint. The system was failing on the demand side: wrong crowd, wrong siting, wrong incentives. So the US$10M goes to demand, not steel.

01 · DIAGNOSE

A MECE funnel, not a wish list

Structured every possible reason consumers skip pickup into four non-overlapping drivers — Awareness, Motivation, Access, Experience. Tested each against evidence; retained four, disproved one ("not enough boxes").

02 · THE SYSTEM INSIGHT

The system punished the behaviour it wanted

Full lockers, 3-day deadlines and a 2-strike blacklist penalised the exact habit Shopee needed to grow. Fix: flip the stick into a carrot — "collect this week → free boba", partner-funded, with grace instead of bans.

// How I think

Three habits, nine years of practice

These are habits I've built the slow way — through years of problems where the visible symptom and the actual cause live in different departments. Each one comes with a real example.

Δ

Distrust the obvious answer

The first explanation everyone agrees on is a hypothesis, not a fact. I test it before spending money on it.

Receipt: "Toilets smell → clean harder" was wrong. I traced it through technical drawings to the ventilation system — a hard-services fault masquerading as a soft-services one.
⇄

Follow the incentives

Systems produce exactly the behaviour their incentives reward. When outcomes look irrational, someone's incentive is mis-wired.

Receipt: Grew estate occupancy 68.5% → 74.6% in a year — not by discounting, but by fixing the marketing, engagement and vacancy loop that kept units invisible.
∑

Make the data confess

Fault trends, SOR line items, KPI reports — the system's real story is in its data exhaust, if you bother to read it.

Receipt: Caught measurement errors and rate discrepancies in contractor quotations by checking them line-by-line against the Schedule of Rates — before approval, not after payment.
